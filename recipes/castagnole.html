Castagnole




Description

Of Romagna origin, they are small pancakes that are fragrant on the outside and
soft on the inside, rolled in granulated sugar.
This cake is typical of carnival, and the most classic version involves the use of anise.


Ingredients

    Ingredients for approximately 30 castagnole

  • Butter => 40g
  • Eggs => 2
  • Lemon peel => ½
  • Salt => a pinch
  • Vanilla pod => 1
  • Flour 00 => 200g
  • Sugar => 50g
  • Anise liquor => 1 spoon
  • Baking powder for cakes => 8g

    • For sprinkle

  • Sugar to taste

    • For frying

  • Peanut seed oil

Tips

  • Before forming the balls you can let the dough rest for about 30 minutes. This way the flavors will blend perfectly.
  • If you do not like the taste of aniseed you can use schnapps, rum or orange juice.
  • Use powdered sugar mixed with a little cinnamon instead of granulated sugar.

Steps

  1. Cut the vanilla pod and take out the seeds, then add them to the sugar 1 and mix.
  2. In another bowl, place flour, add sugar mixed with vanilla seeds and eggs.
  3. Add the butter, grated lemon peel and anise liquor.
  4. Add a pinch of salt and baking powder.
  5. Start mixing the ingredients with a fork and continue by kneading by hand.
  6. As soon as you have an uniform mixture, transfer it to a lightly floured work surface.
    Knead until you have a smooth, soft dough.
  7. At this point begin to heat the oil, which should reach a temperature of 170°,
    only in this way will you get castagnole golden just right and cooked on the inside.
  8. Move on to forming the castagnole.
    Take some dough from the bowl and make a loaf on a lightly floured surface.
    Then cut out small pieces of dough about 12 g. With this dough you will get about 30.
    Then shape each portion with your hands to make small balls.
  9. As soon as the oil is hot, dip a few pieces at a time;
    you can help with a skimmer to transfer them without distorting them.
    Turn the castagnole often with a skimmer to encourage even cooking.
    Then drain your castagnole.
  10. Transfer them to a sheet of straw paper to remove excess oil.
    Place the sugar in a bowl and roll the chestnuts inside the sugar.
  11. Continue in this way for the others and serve your castagnole.

Food preservation

It is recommended that the castagnole be eaten freshly, or at most within the day.
The dough can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 1 day.
Freezing is not recommended.


About castagnole

The recipe for castagnole is very old: an eighteenth-century manuscript volume was found in the state archives
of Viterbo in which four recipes for castagnole are described, one of which involves baking.

In the Italian tradition, castagnole are considered one of the symbols of Carnival,
as is also attested by a proverb widespread in the various Marche dialects.
It is given below:

«Fenito Carnevà, fenito amore
fenito a fà la pachia da signore
fenito de stacià farina in fiore
fenito de magnà le castagnole»
(IT)

«Finished carnival, finished love
finished the gentleman's pact
finished sifting flour in bloom
finished eating castagnole»
